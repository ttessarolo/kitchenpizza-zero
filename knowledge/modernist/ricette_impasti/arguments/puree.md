# Puree Recipes for Flavor Variations

<!-- p.98-99 -->

These purees are designed to be mixed into pizza dough as flavor variations. They replace an equal amount of water in the dough recipe. See the Flavor Variations tables (p.94-97) for specific amounts per pizza style.

## 1. Sunchoke Puree

**Yield:** 475 g (1¾ cups + 2 Tbsp)

| Ingredient | Weight | Volume | Scaling % |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sunchokes, peeled and cut into large dice | 250 g | 5 to 6 ea | 100 |
| Olive oil | 12.5 g | 2¾ tsp | 5 |
| Fine salt | 2.5 g | ½ tsp | 1 |
| Water | 415 g | 1¾ cups | 166 |

**Method:** Toss sunchokes with oil and salt. Roast at 220°C / 425°F until golden brown, about 15 min, turning halfway. Cool. Blend with 250 g of the water until smooth. Pass through fine sieve. Combine 165 g (¾ cup) puree with remaining water. Store leftover puree frozen up to 3 mo.

## 2. Grilled Corn Puree

**Yield:** 670 g (2¼ cups)

| Ingredient | Weight | Volume | Scaling % |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sweet yellow corn on the cob | 1.2 kg | 4 ears | 100 |
| Vegetable oil | as needed | | |
| Water | 335 g | 1⅓ cups | 27.91 |

**Method:** Husk corn, brush with thin layer of oil. Grill on high heat 5-7 min, turning every 30 s for even char. Cool. Cut kernels (~335 g / 2 cups cooked). Blend with water until smooth. Pass through fine sieve. Cool over ice bath.

## 3. Pressure-Caramelized Shiitake Mushroom Puree

**Yield:** 550 g (2 cups)

| Ingredient | Weight | Volume | Scaling % |
|---|---|---|---|
| Butter | 60 g | ¼ cup | 24 |
| Shiitake mushrooms, cleaned, stemmed, thinly sliced | 250 g | 4¼ cups | 100 |
| Fine salt | 2.5 g | ½ tsp | 1 |
| Baking soda | 1.25 g | ¼ tsp | 0.5 |
| Water | 265 g | 1 cup + 2 Tbsp | 106 |

### For Pressure Cooker
Melt butter in pressure cooker over medium heat. Add mushrooms, salt, and baking soda; stir to distribute. Add 30 g (2 Tbsp) water. Pressure-cook at 1 bar / 15 psi for 30 min. Quick-release by running tepid water over rim. Blend with remaining water until smooth. Pass through fine sieve. Cool to 21°C / 70°F. Store frozen up to 3 mo.

### For Instapot
Set to Saute. Melt butter. Add mushrooms, salt, baking soda; stir. Add 30 g water. Switch to Pressure Cook, 12 psi (High), 1 h. Lock lid. When depressurized, cool. Complete puree steps above.

## 4. Pressure-Caramelized Cauliflower Puree

**Yield:** 575 g (2½ cups)

| Ingredient | Weight | Volume | Scaling % |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cauliflower florets, 1.25 cm / 1¼ in thick | 600 g | 6 cups | 100 |
| Olive oil | 10 g | 2 tsp | 1.67 |
| Baking soda | 3 g | ½ tsp | 0.5 |
| Water | 360 g | 1½ cups + 1 Tbsp | 60 |

### For Pressure Cooker
Combine cauliflower, oil, and baking soda in pressure cooker. Lightly sweat. Pressure-cook at 1 bar / 15 psi for 30 min. Quick-release. Blend until smooth, pass through fine sieve. Combine 150 g (½ cup + 2 Tbsp) cauliflower puree with water. Store frozen up to 3 mo.

### For Instapot
Set to Saute. Combine cauliflower, oil, baking soda; stir. Lightly sweat. Switch to Pressure Cook, 12 psi (High), 1 h. Lock lid. When depressurized, cool. Complete puree steps above.

## Notes

In some cases, these puree recipes yield more than what you need for 1 kg of dough because making small quantities is more difficult than making a large batch and freezing the leftover puree.
