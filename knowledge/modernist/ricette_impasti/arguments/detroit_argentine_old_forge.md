# Detroit, Argentinean, & Old Forge Pizza Dough Recipes

<!-- p.74-79 -->

## 1. Detroit-Style Pizza Dough (Master Recipe) <!-- p.75 -->

**Type:** Master Recipe | **Time:** Active 15-20 min / Inactive 3½ h | **Yield:** ~1 kg

| Ingredient | Weight | Baker's % |
|---|---|---|
| Water, 30-30.5°C / 85-87°F | 405 g | 70.43 |
| Bread flour, 11.5%-12% protein | 490 g | 85.22 |
| Semolina flour | 85 g | 14.78 |
| Fine salt | 11.5 g | 2 |
| Sugar | 8 g | 1.39 |
| Instant dry yeast | 5.2 g | 0.9 |

**Net Contents:** Bread flour 490 g (85.22%), Semolina flour 85 g (14.78%), Water 405 g (70.43%), Salt 11.5 g (2%), Sugar 8 g (1.39%), Yeast 5.2 g (0.9%)

**Flour:** Ceresota/Heckers Unbleached All-Purpose Flour.

**Key:** Warm water (30-30.5°C). Mixed to just UNDER full gluten development. No fat in dough (oil used for pan coating).

**Process:** Mix -> Generously oiled tub, turn to coat -> Bench rest 15 min -> Four-edge fold -> Bench rest 15 min -> Oil Detroit pans -> Divide (500 g for full, 330 g for half) -> Preshape in pan -> Proof 3 h at 21°C or 2½ h at 27°C/65% RH; stretch after first hour.

**Pan sizes:** Full: 35 cm by 25 cm / 14 in by 10 in; Half: 25 cm by 20 cm / 10 in by 8 in. Blue steel recommended.

| Ingredient | % | Add to |

Kiwi juice and papaya juice performed best in tests.

## 3. Argentinean-Style "Al Molde" Pizza Dough <!-- p.77-78 -->

**Type:** Submaster Recipe | **Time:** Active 15-20 min / Inactive 2½ h | **Yield:** ~1 kg

| Ingredient | Weight | Baker's % |
|---|---|---|
| Water, 30-30.5°C / 85-87°F | 400 g | 67.8 |
| All-purpose flour, 10%-11.5% protein | 490 g | 83.05 |
| Cake flour | 100 g | 16.95 |
| Fine salt | 12 g | 2.03 |
| Instant dry yeast | 5.5 g | 0.93 |

**Net Contents:** All-purpose flour 490 g (83.05%), Cake flour 100 g (16.95%), Water 400 g (67.8%), Salt 12 g (2.03%), Yeast 5.5 g (0.93%)

Mix to just under full gluten development. Bench rest 15 min -> Divide 500 g -> Ball -> Oil two 35 cm / 14 in pizza pans -> Bench rest 20 min -> Shape in pans -> Proof 2 h at 21°C or 1¼ h at 27°C/65% RH.

Versatile dough, can also be used for Burt Katz-inspired pizza (page 237).

## 4. Old Forge Pizza Dough <!-- p.79 -->

**Type:** Submaster Recipe | **Time:** Active 20-25 min / Inactive 6 h | **Yield:** ~1 kg

| Ingredient | Weight | Baker's % |
|---|---|---|
| Water, 21°C | 355 g | 59.17 |
| Instant dry yeast | 4 g | 0.67 |
| Flour, 11.5%-14% protein | 600 g | 100 |
| Fine salt | 12 g | 2 |
| Sugar | 18 g | 3 |
| Extra-virgin olive oil | 12 g | 2 |

**Net Contents:** Flour 600 g (100%), Water 355 g (59.17%), Sugar 18 g (3%), Salt 12 g (2%), Fat 12 g (2%), Yeast 4 g (0.67%)

**Flour:** General Mills All Trumps, Tony Gemignani CA Artisan Type 00, Bouncer Premium High Gluten, Pillsbury Balancer Hi Gluten, or Grain Craft Power High Gluten.

**Process:** Mix with autolyse 30 min -> Add salt -> Add sugar and oil in slow stream -> Mix to medium gluten development -> Oiled tub, four-edge fold -> Bulk ferment 2½ h with 4 folds to full gluten development -> Rest 30 min -> Oil pan(s) -> Preshape in pan -> Proof 3 h at 21°C or 2 h at 27°C/65% RH; cold-proof 16 h at 4°C, then 2 h at 27°C/65% RH.

**Sizes:** 46 cm by 33 cm / 18 in by 13 in (do not divide) or 33 cm by 23 cm / 13 in by 9 in (500 g).

A whole Old Forge Pizza is sometimes colloquially referred to as a "tray."
