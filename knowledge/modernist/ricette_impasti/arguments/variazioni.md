# Dough Variations (Cross-Style)

<!-- p.78-97 -->

These variation tables allow you to create a multitude of doughs based on the master recipes. Follow the methods in the master recipes.

## 1. Levain-Raised Variations (p.80-81)

Replace the poolish (or yeast in direct recipes) with liquid levain. Levain amounts by style:

| Pizza Style | Levain Weight | Levain % | Flour Weight | Flour % |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Thin-Crust (p.26) | 160 g | 30.77 | 520 g | 100 |
| Brazilian Thin-Crust (p.30) | 180 g | 34.29 | Cake 370 g + Bread 155 g | 100 |
| Deep-Dish (p.34) | 120 g | 37.5 | 320 g | 100 |
| Neapolitan (p.38) | 130 g | 24.3 | 535 g | 100 |
| New York (p.45) | 140 g | 26.42 | 530 g | 100 |
| Artisan (p.54) | 150 g | 27.27 | 550 g | 100 |
| Focaccia (p.60) | 190 g | 47.5 | 400 g | 100 |
| NY Square (p.64) | 90 g | 25.35 | 355 g | 100 |
| Detroit (p.75) | 150 g | 30 | Bread 415 g + Semolina 85 g | 100 |

**Instructions:** Replace poolish with levain. Omit yeast (for styles with poolish). For Brazilian and Deep-Dish (direct styles), replace yeast with levain.

## 3. Compleat Wheat Variations (p.82-85)

Adds wheat bran and wheat germ back to create a whole-grain version. Each style has specific amounts for bran/germ mixture soaked in water.

**Key parameters vary by style.** Example for Thin-Crust:
- Flour: 465 g (100%), Water: 395 g (84.95%), Yeast: 4.2 g (0.9%), Salt: 11 g (2.37%)
- Bran/Germ: Wheat bran 65 g (13.98%), Wheat germ 10 g (2.15%), Water 75 g
- Cornmeal: 50 g (10.75%)

## 4. Grain, Nut, or Seed Variations (p.86-87)

Add 20-25% (of flour weight) grain, nut, or seed inclusions. Mix dough to medium (not full) gluten development, then add inclusions on low speed. Bulk ferment 2¼ h with 4 four-edge folds to full gluten development.

**Inclusion amounts by style (approximate % of flour):**

| Style | Inclusion Weight | Inclusion % |
|---|---|---|
| Thin-Crust | 110 g | 22.45 |
| Neapolitan | 110 g | 20.37 |
| New York | 100 g | 20.62 |
| Artisan | 110 g | 22.22 |
| Focaccia | 100 g | 25 |
| NY Square | 75 g | 23.44 |
| Al Taglio | 130 g | 22.61 |
| Detroit | 105 g | 20 |

**Notes:** Soak, sprout, or cook grains first. Toast nuts (chop large ones first). Cool hot preparations before adding. Max dimension 3 mm / ¼ in for thin-crust. Can use grano arso for Neapolitan.

## 5. Country-Style Variations (p.88)

Substitute 30% of total flour weight: 15% whole wheat flour + 15% rye flour.

**Example for Thin-Crust (540 g total flour):**
- All-purpose/bread flour: 380 g (2¾ cups) ~70%
- Whole wheat flour: 80 g (⅔ cup) ~15%
- Light/medium/dark rye flour: 80 g (¾ cup) ~15%

Can replace both with 30% whole wheat only. For Neapolitan, can use 30% masa harina.

## 6. Ancient Grain Variations (p.89)

Substitute 40% of flour with ancient grains:
- All-purpose/bread flour: ~60%
- Whole spelt flour: ~15%
- Einkorn flour: ~15%
- Emmer flour: ~10%

Alternative: khorasan flour (15%) and buckwheat flour (15%) can replace einkorn and emmer.

## 7. No-Knead Variations (p.90-91)

Minimal mixing with long fermentation. Details specific to each style in original tables.

## 8. Daily Pizza Variations (p.92-93)

Large batches designed for making one pizza per day for up to 10-14 days. Key additions:
- Ascorbic acid (~0.22-0.27% of flour) for dough preservation
- Polydextrose (1% in thin-crust) or extra fat

**Process:** Combine all ingredients, mix on low speed 2-3 min -> Bulk ferment 1 h at 21°C -> Plastic wrap on surface -> Refrigerate at least 23 h -> Divide daily -> Proof 4½-5½ h at 21°C (3-4 h for Neapolitan).

**Yields by style (for ~10 days):**

| Style | Total Flour | Total Yield |
|---|---|---|
| Thin-Crust | 1.97 kg | ~3.5 kg |
| Brazilian | 2.21 kg | ~3.7 kg |
| Neapolitan | 2.1 kg | ~3.7 kg |
| New York | 4.2 kg | ~7.3 kg |
| Artisan | 2.06 kg | ~3.6 kg |
